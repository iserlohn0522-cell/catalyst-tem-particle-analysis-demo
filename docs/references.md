# Related Work Notes

This file collects references that frame the public demo. The list is intentionally selective; it is not a full literature review.

## Closest Catalyst and Nanoparticle Work

1. Genc, A. et al. "A versatile machine learning workflow for high-throughput analysis of supported metal catalyst particles." Ultramicroscopy 271, 114116 (2025). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2025.114116

   Closest detector-to-SAM supported catalyst workflow. This demo cites it as related work and does not use its code, data, images, README text, or weights.

2. Zhong, X. et al. "Automated Particle Size Analysis of Supported Nanoparticle TEM Images Using a Pre-Trained SAM Model." Nanomaterials 15, 1886 (2025). https://doi.org/10.3390/nano15241886

   Direct supported-nanoparticle TEM context for SAM-based particle size analysis.

3. Monteiro, G. A. A. et al. "Pre-trained artificial intelligence-aided analysis of nanoparticles using the segment anything model." Scientific Reports 15, 2341 (2025). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-86327-x

   Useful comparator for pretrained-SAM nanoparticle segmentation and morphology measurement.

4. Treder, K. P. et al. "nNPipe: a neural network pipeline for automated analysis of morphologically diverse catalyst systems." npj Computational Materials 9, 18 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41524-022-00949-7

   Catalyst-focused automated morphology pipeline and direct context for complex catalyst support images.

5. Sun, Z. et al. "A deep learning-based framework for automatic analysis of the nanoparticle morphology in SEM/TEM images." Nanoscale 14, 10761-10772 (2022). https://doi.org/10.1039/D2NR01029A

   Direct nanoparticle morphology automation context across SEM/TEM images.

## Measurement and Reporting Context

6. Aviles, K. M. and Lear, B. J. "Practical Guide to Automated TEM Image Analysis for Increased Accuracy and Precision in the Measurement of Particle Size and Morphology." ACS Nanoscience Au 5, 117-127 (2025). https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnanoscienceau.4c00076

   Practical motivation for automated TEM particle-size and morphology measurement.

7. Meyer, R. J. et al. "Recommendations to standardize reporting, execution and interpretation of STEM/TEM measurements." Journal of Catalysis 433, 115480 (2024). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcat.2024.115480

   Reporting and interpretation context for catalyst TEM/STEM measurements.

8. Cervera Gontard, L., Ozkaya, D. and Dunin-Borkowski, R. E. "A simple algorithm for measuring particle size distributions on an uneven background from TEM images." Ultramicroscopy 111, 101-106 (2011). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2010.10.011

   Classic context for uneven-background TEM particle measurement.

9. House, S. D., Chen, Y., Jin, R. and Yang, J. C. "High-throughput, semi-automated quantitative STEM mass measurement of supported metal nanoparticles using a conventional TEM/STEM." Ultramicroscopy 182, 145-155 (2017). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2017.07.004

   High-throughput supported-metal nanoparticle measurement context.

10. Schneider, C. A., Rasband, W. S. and Eliceiri, K. W. "NIH Image to ImageJ: 25 years of image analysis." Nature Methods 9, 671-675 (2012). https://doi.org/10.1038/nmeth.2089

   Background for the ImageJ/Fiji microscopy analysis lineage.

## Model and Tool Lineage

11. Kirillov, A. et al. "Segment Anything." ICCV (2023). https://doi.org/10.1109/ICCV51070.2023.00371

   Foundation reference for SAM as a promptable segmentation model.

12. Archit, A. et al. "Segment Anything for Microscopy." Nature Methods 22, 579-591 (2025). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-024-02580-4

   Microscopy-specific foundation-model context.

13. Ronneberger, O., Fischer, P. and Brox, T. "U-Net: Convolutional Networks for Biomedical Image Segmentation." MICCAI (2015). https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-24574-4_28

   Segmentation model lineage.

14. He, K. et al. "Mask R-CNN." ICCV (2017). https://doi.org/10.1109/ICCV.2017.322

   Instance segmentation model lineage.

15. Redmon, J. et al. "You Only Look Once: Unified, Real-Time Object Detection." CVPR (2016). https://doi.org/10.1109/CVPR.2016.91

   Object detector lineage.

## Scale and Reproducibility

16. Meng, S. et al. "Automatic extraction of scale information for interactive measurement of anything in microscopy images." Knowledge-Based Systems 324, 113578 (2025). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.knosys.2025.113578

   Scale extraction context for measurement reproducibility.
